Aim

This program aims to provide a broad and fundamental understanding of the theory and practice of computers and information technology and prepares graduates for a professional career in computing and the software development and information services industry.


Entry requirements

To be eligible for selection, applicants are required to have:

  Completed the South Australian Certificate of Education (SACE); and

  • completed 200 credits of subjects;
  • achieved a grade of C* or better in the Personal Learning Plan, 20 credits of literacy, 10 credits of numeracy and the Research Project at Stage 2;
  • achieved a grade C- or better in an additional 60 credits at Stage 2;
  • obtained a competitive ATAR.

  * For Stage 2 subjects a grade of C- or better is required


Alternatively, applicants are required to have one of the following:
  • Completed the International Baccalaureate Diploma;
  • Completed or partly completed a recognised higher education program at a recognised higher education institution;
  • Completed a recognised diploma or associated diploma from TAFE or Certificate IV level course;
  • Completed at least four Open Universities Australia (OUA) courses at the appropriate level;
  • Qualified for Special Entry;
  • Completed University Foundation program;

OR

Completed one of the following professional courses:

  • Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer (MCSE)
  • MCSE + Internet and Microsoft Certified Solution Developer (MCSD)
  • Lotus Certified Professional (CLP)
  • Novell Certified NetWare Engineer (CNE)
  • Cisco Certified Networking Associate (CCNA)
  • Cisco Certified Networking Professional (CCNP)

Content and structure

The program consists of:
Core courses in information technology science (67.5 units),
An Elective course (4.5 units)
A major (36 units) or two minors (2 x 18 units) to provide specialisation. There are four defined majors to provide (named) specialisations in Information Technology, however a student may take a combination of two IT minors from the list at the end of the program schedule, or a combination of an IT minor from the list at the end of the program schedule plus a minor from the list of minors provided by Schools from across the university.
A total of 108 units are required for the completion of the program.

Rules

  1. The program consists of: Core courses in information technology (67.5 units), Specialisation through a software development major (36 units) An Elective course (4.5 units) that can be selected from a list of approved university wide Elective courses.
  2. A total of 108 units are required for the completion of the program.

Notes

  1. The program is available at the Mawson Lakes Campus, however some courses are also offered at City West and Magill.
  2. The program can be studied full-time and part-time internal, and part-time external (first two years only).
  3. The Elective course can be selected from any School within the University.
